Pre-flight check (mandatory)

Activation is fail-closed. If required evidence is missing or unverifiable, the process must stop.

  • you understand GitJoker-C2 is non-offensive governance infrastructure
  • you accept that execution without validation is not allowed
  • you are activating an IPR scope: personal, organizational, or institutional
Fail-closed rule: uncertainty is treated as risk → FAIL_CLOSED.

Step 1 — Choose IPR scope

IPR-PERSONAL

For individuals and professionals who must make accountable digital decisions.

  • human root attribution (IPR/HRR)
  • standard DGV outcomes
  • evidence bundle for verification

IPR-ORGANIZATIONAL

For organizations that require governance across people, roles and processes.

  • role/scope governance
  • policy-based DGV
  • audit-ready evidence workflows

IPR-INSTITUTIONAL

For public institutions and high-criticality workflows requiring long-term verification.

  • strict fail-closed governance
  • high-assurance evidence chain
  • optional cryptographic anchoring (policy-driven)
The IPR defines legitimacy and responsibility. GitJoker-C2 enforces governance before execution.

Step 2 — Select access level

BASE

  • minimal chain enabled
  • standard validation
  • evidence bundle generation

OPPONIBLE / STRONG

  • advanced DGV policies
  • audit-ready evidence workflows
  • high-criticality options (anchors, LTA readiness)
Outcomes: ALLOW · REVIEW · DENY · FAIL_CLOSED

Step 3 — Activation outputs

After activation, GitJoker-C2 can produce structured, verifiable outputs for governed actions:

  • IPR/HRR status and scope proof
  • DGV decision outcome and policy reference
  • certified time linkage
  • evidence bundle (recompute and match)
  • optional critical anchors (when required)
Golden rule: verification is recompute and match, not “trust the website”.

Institutional & enterprise onboarding

For pilots, audits, or partnerships, activation requires an explicit governance scope and risk profile.

  • define use-case and criticality
  • define policy set for DGV
  • define evidence retention and verification requirements
  • enable optional anchors only when required
Execution without a defined, verifiable policy set is non-conformant.
