What can be verified

  • IPR/HRR: existence and legitimacy of the human root
  • DGV outcome: ALLOW / REVIEW / DENY / FAIL_CLOSED
  • Time linkage: consistent temporal reference
  • Evidence Bundle: hashes, signatures and metadata
  • Optional anchors: external timestamping/anchoring when enabled
Fail-closed rule: if a required element is missing or unverifiable, the result must be FAIL_CLOSED.

Verification workflow

Step 1 — Obtain the Evidence Bundle

Collect the evidence bundle (hashes, signatures, time reference, metadata) from the governed action. Verification starts from the bundle, not from a webpage claim.

Step 2 — Recompute hashes

Recompute cryptographic hashes from the original payload/data and compare them to the bundle values.

recompute(payload) → hash → compare → match / mismatch
Mismatch = integrity failure → FAIL_CLOSED.
Step 3 — Verify signatures and attribution

Verify signatures and attribution claims: the bundle must be tied to a valid IPR/HRR identity root and an authorized decision scope.

Missing or invalid attribution → FAIL_CLOSED.
Step 4 — Validate DGV outcome

Confirm the decision outcome recorded by DGV and its policy reference, if applicable.

ALLOW · REVIEW · DENY · FAIL_CLOSED
Step 5 — Validate time linkage

Verify that the time reference is consistent, documented and matches the evidence bundle metadata.

Unverifiable time source or inconsistent timestamps → FAIL_CLOSED.
Step 6 — Optional: verify external anchors

If anchors are enabled, verify that the anchored hash matches the bundle hash. Anchors are activated only for high-criticality cases.

Anchors do not replace verification: they strengthen long-term opponibility.
